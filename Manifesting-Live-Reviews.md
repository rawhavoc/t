<h2 id="pitfalls-and-how-to-avoid-them">Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them</h2>
<p>Even the best system has its flaws.<br>I’ve encountered a few pitfalls along the way when relying on live reviews, and here’s what I’ve learned to steer clear of common traps.</p>
<h3 id="-common-pitfalls-"><strong>Common Pitfalls:</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Overwhelming Volume:</strong><br>Too much feedback can be paralyzing.</li>
<li><strong>Biased Opinions:</strong><br>Some reviews might have an agenda, whether it’s overly positive or negative.</li>
<li><strong>Outdated Information:</strong><br>Reviews that aren’t updated might misrepresent the current state of a product.</li>
<li><strong>Fake Engagement:</strong><br>Be aware of review bombing and manipulated feedback.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="-how-to-avoid-these-traps-"><strong>How to Avoid These Traps:</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Filter by Date:</strong><br>Always check when a review was posted.<br>Recent feedback is usually more reliable.</li>
<li><strong>Look for Consistency:</strong><br>Don’t focus on a single comment—see what the majority is saying.</li>
<li><strong>Use Multiple Sources:</strong><br>Cross-check information from different review platforms.</li>
<li><strong>Engage Directly:</strong><br>Ask questions in the community if something seems off.</li>
<li><strong>Trust Verified Users:</strong><br>Verified reviewers or long-standing community members are usually more reliable.</li>
</ul>
